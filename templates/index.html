Creating Strong Passwords

Follow these guidelines to create secure passwords:

  • Use at least 12 characters, preferably 16 or more
  • Avoid common dictionary words and predictable patterns
  • Don't use sequential characters (abc, 123, qwerty)
  • Include a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Use random or uncommon combinations rather than personal information
  • Avoid repeating the same character multiple times
  • Don't use your username, email, or personal details
  • Consider using a password manager to generate and store unique passwords
  • Never reuse passwords across different accounts or services
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible for additional security
